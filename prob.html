The Problem

Bad eating habits have become a serious issue today, especially among children and teenagers. Many young people eat too much unhealthy food such as fast food, sugary snacks, and processed meals. These foods are often high in fat, sugar, and salt but low in nutrients. Because they are cheap, tasty, and easy to buy, kids choose them over healthier options. Another problem is the consumption of unclean or unsafe food, which can cause stomach infections, food poisoning, and long-term health issues.

Malnutrition is also a major concern. Some children become undernourished because they do not get enough vitamins, minerals, and protein. This can lead to being underweight, weak immune systems, slower growth, and difficulty concentrating in school. On the other hand, some children become overweight or obese due to eating too much junk food and not having a balanced diet. Obesity can lead to serious conditions such as diabetes, heart problems, and low self-esteem.

These problems often come from a lack of education about healthy eating, busy family schedules, or financial difficulties that make it hard to buy nutritious food. Many kids also follow what they see online or from friends, which often promotes unhealthy choices. Because of these issues, it is important to raise awareness about proper nutrition, teach children how to make better food choices, and help families access cleaner and healthier food. Your advocacy aims to address these problems and encourage better eating habits for a healthier future.

How can we stay healthy?

1. Eat more fruits and vegetables

- Include colorful fruits and vegetables in every meal for vitamins and fiber.

- Snack on fruits instead of chips or sweets.

2. Drink water and limit sugary drinks

- Water keeps you hydrated and gives energy.

- Avoid soda, energy drinks, and sweetened juices.

3. Avoid too much fast food and snacks

- Limit chips, candy, fried foods, and instant noodles.

- Choose healthier snacks like nuts, yogurt, or fruit.

4. Include protein and whole grains

- Eat eggs, chicken, fish, beans, rice, or whole-grain bread.

- Proteins help your body grow, and whole grains give lasting energy.
